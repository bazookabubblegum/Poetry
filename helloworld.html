Poetry of Nonsense

Alone At Last

How could they know what tides would soon blow,
In ancient vacations the mind should not go,
Restorational invasion of old implications,
Foundations of our senses and mental stations,
To be at the end of a double edged sword,
Wander the mountains till true self is forged,
Forget what you know, for it does no good
Alive in ourselves, a will firm like wood.
